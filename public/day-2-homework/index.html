⚠ Don't skip this part

Each talking point has a Control Point.

The Control Point is the one idea you're hammering home in the homeowner's mind during that talking point. Neal covers what control points are and why they're crucial on Day 1 (training page 21). Lock in the control point — that's the takeaway the homeowner has to leave the room with.

Control-point QUESTIONS are the most important part.

Phrase your question so the homeowner thinks they said it themselves. But to make the summary actually land, you have to first dig out their reasoning — most homeowners will just say "it's important" to the opening question, which gives you nothing to summarize. So you ALWAYS follow with "why is it important?" That's where the real content comes from. Then you summarize their reasoning back using your control point as the words you put in their mouth.

Full flow — 15 Years in Business

1. You ask the importance question: "When dealing with a roofing company, how important is it to you that they're likely to be around in the future if you have a need or something goes wrong?"

2. They short-answer: "It's important." ← this is normal. Don't accept it — push for the WHY.

3. You ask the WHY: "Why is that important to you?"

4. NOW they give you the real reasoning — in their own words. Let them talk.

5. You summarize back: "So if I understand you correctly, you're saying experience matters and is important to you?" ← They agree. Now they said it.

The "why" question is non-negotiable. Without it, the homeowner has nothing invested in the answer and your summary feels like words you stuffed in their mouth. With it, they've already laid out the reasoning — your summary just gives that reasoning a name.

Do this on every single one of the 4 talking points. Don't tell them the control point — get them to say it back.

Slide 9 — Our Install Process

Yes, we pull permits. Why? It's what reputable companies do. I can't tell you how many times we get a call because a homeowner paid chuck in a truck for an inexpensive roof. Then to find out their insurance company sends them a notice later asking them to replace the new roof they just paid for. You see Insurance companies go by permits. So now they must pay twice. Did they save any money, you tell me?

We take pictures, lots and lots of pictures. We document everything. It keeps everything professional. Homeowners will walk around after the job is done and point out damaged screens and ask us to replace them. To the photos we go, only to discover they were damaged prior to us being there. Simply put, when was the last time you walked all around your home doing a thorough inspection? Same thing happens with the drive — they never noticed the big crack before we put the dump trailer there. So naturally we damaged the driveway, until we break out the photos. It keeps everyone responsible which is just the best policy, don't you agree?

We will assign a Jobsite foreman to you and your home. He is there for EVERYTHING install related. You will get his cell phone, and you can't possibly call him too much. If you need anything at all, please call them 24/7, 7 days a week. Their single job responsibility is to make sure you have the best possible experience!

Unlike other companies we do not use dumpsters. They can seriously damage your driveway. We only use dump trailers which roll in on tires and roll away on tires leaving no chance of damaging your driveway.

We always strip the old shingles off. Yes, a roof-over is permitted once by the building department but it voids the manufacturer warranty, and it cuts the life of the shingle in half. We just will not do that kind of work for our customers. Do you want a warranty? Do you want the roof to last its entire designed life?

Instead of using felt paper or even synthetic underlayment we put peel and stick on all our roofs. It's self-sealing for one which prevents leaks but it's also much safer here in Florida as we could have a pop-up thunderstorm at any time. Felt can blow off before the main shingles are applied. With peel and stick you couldn't pull it off if you wanted to. So, you get a better-quality roof and some peace of mind during the install.

Here is the only thing we don't know for sure: rotted decking and/or fascia. Both must be replaced per building code and of course you don't want us putting new material over rotted wood. You are responsible for any rotted wood we find. We will take pictures of all of it so you can always see what was damaged and what we replaced. Normally if you don't have any stains in your ceilings you should not require much if any wood to be replaced, maybe a sheet or two. I'll show you that pricing when we get to that part.

We do our best to clean up everything, to leave your yard better than we found it. However, a reroof is the messiest project you could ever do at your home. We sweep, we use magnets, we sweep some more and go around your yard with rolling magnets. It's just impossible to get them all — there are over 10,000 nails in your average roof. So please, if we reroof your home, don't go barefoot in the yard for a couple weeks.

Lastly, once the job is finished that Job Site foreman you've become friends with is going to walk around the house with you to get your approval that you are happy with everything. Slide 11 — Metal — Colors &Amp; Emissivity

These are all the Metal colors that you can choose from. Have you ever heard the word emissivity? It's a fancy word that means (point to your iPad) if this was out in the sun it would get hot right? When I take it out of the sun how long is it going to stay hot? The lower the number the more it absorbs the heat. To give you an idea, the shingles you have on your home are only rated at 4% emissivity.

Basically, if I go on your roof tomorrow morning before the sun comes up it will still be warm from the day before. Now out of all the colors on this page which do you think would be the hottest? (They always pick black) Absolutely! But with the metal and the special paint what is the emissivity? (point to the E) Yeah, 84% compared to 4%. Now you can get the color you want without the fear it is going to make things hotter. Based on that which color do you think you would really want, knowing you don't have to worry about that?

One thing to point out about exposed fastener are the screws. This is our lifetime screw. Older screws used metal washers which due to thermal expansion would cause the screws to come loose, or they used rubber washers which would dry rot and have issues. We use the ZXL lifetime screw with color matching caps, so you can't see the screws from a distance and yet the neoprene washer will not dry rot or suffer from thermal expansion, creating a lasting roof that doesn't leak.

The difference between the Exposed Fastener and the Standing Seam is that the standing seam's screws are hidden under the metal, but the wind rating isn't as high as the exposed fastener system and the Standing Seam is about 25% more expensive. You really just need to decide what's most important to you when choosing between these two. You can't go wrong with either of them.

Slides 17–21 — Energy Saver Package

Speaking of radiant heat, what would you say your average electric bill is in the summer? Now how about in the winter? Ok so then overall you'd say your average bill is about ________?

I'm asking because for most of our customers we do an energy package which helps them cover the cost of the roof. In other words, if I could on a cash-flow basis make your new roof nearly free, you might add me to the Christmas Card list?

Here in Florida about 70% of your electric bill is because of air conditioning. So, it would stand to reason if we can keep your AC from working hard or at all then we can cut your bill way down, right? See, up north they get to run ductwork in the basement where it's cool, down here we run all our duct work in the attic where it's crazy hot.

In the summer attic temperatures can reach 150 degrees or higher. Ever been up there in the summer? You have like 12 seconds and then you can't breathe. Well, what that means is that you are PAYING, with your hard-earned money, to cool the air to 50 degrees because you're about to pipe it up into the furnace so that when it finally comes out over here it's 74 degrees. Yes, I know that makes zero sense but that's the downside to how we build here.

Now there is a solution, and we've helped LOTS of homeowners fix their houses already. I know what you're thinking — "this is going to be expensive" or "money is tight right now." Well don't worry because the fix I'm going to show you isn't going to cost you any more than you are already paying right now! In fact, as I said earlier, I won't ask you for a single penny ever.

The solution is 3 parts. First is to add more insulation as most homes don't have anywhere near enough for our Florida heat. We install R-38 or about this much (use your hands to show about 15") compared to what most people have which is this much (use your hands to show about 4"). I'll pop my head up there in a minute to double check what you have.

The second is to seal the ducts you have in the attic. Neither the builder nor the guy who installed your AC were concerned about your electric bill — they were more interested in doing the job quickly to get paid and move on. That means your ducts were never sealed properly, so the air you are paying to cool isn't making it into the house but instead getting lost up there. So we will seal and reseal them using sealing mastic. The other issue with improperly sealed ducts is air quality — the return ducts are sucking in dust, pollen, mold and anything else floating around up there.

The third part, which is the most CRITICAL, is something called Radiant Barrier. It's high-tech stuff that literally reflects 97% of the sun's radiating heat from ever entering your attic. It was invented by NASA for the space shuttle. So now instead of having to pay to cool your air to 50 degrees (scroll back) you only need to cool it to about 70 degrees instead (scroll forward), because we will have dramatically reduced the temperature in your attic.

This will not only save you up to 40% or more off your utility bill but it will also extend the life of your AC system. If the AC is running half as much it's going to last longer, isn't it?

Obviously if you have it already, you're good to go, but I've only seen one home so far that had a radiant barrier installed. Let me just check first and then I can give you the breakdown and the investment for just the roof and then what it looks like for the whole package.
