Each talking point has a Control Point.

The Control Point is the one idea you're hammering home in the homeowner's mind during that talking point. Neal covers what control points are and why they're crucial on Day 1 (training page 21). Lock in the control point — that's the takeaway the homeowner has to leave the room with.

1. 15 Years in Business

Control Point Experience Matters

"When dealing with a company for your roof, how important is it to you that they are likely to be around in the future if you have a need or if something goes wrong?" (Most will answer with "it's important.") "Absolutely. Studies show that over 50% of companies fail to make it to 5 years, and 80% of companies fail to make it to their 15th birthday. Since it's important that the company you choose is around when you need them, I have great news: we are now over 15 years old, which should demonstrate that the likelihood of us being around if you need us is extremely high — wouldn't you agree?"

Source: Day 1 Retail Training, page 37

2. Veteran Owned

Control Point Structured & Disciplined → More Credibility

"We are also a Veteran owned company — how important is that to you?" (If they say important, ask them why. It gets them to start selling themselves on us. Worst case, if they say it's not, you can follow up with:) "I figure if we can trust them to protect our freedom, we can certainly trust them to protect our home!"

Source: Day 1 Retail Training, page 38

Additional framing (Neal): "…so by having the characteristics of structured and disciplined, that would give us a little more credibility — wouldn't you agree?"

3. 5 Star Google Reviews

Control Point Social Proof — backs up everything we cover today, AND leads into 3rd Party Finance

"What did you do to research us before today? Would you agree that people are more likely to complain about companies when they had a bad experience than say good things about them when they had a great experience? Well, the good news is we have tons of 5 Star reviews, so you will find that everything we talk about today will be backed up by our rating on Google."

Source: Day 1 Retail Training, page 39

3a. The Consumer-Vetting Transition

Control Point We Are Limited As Consumers — bridge from Reviews into Finance

"As consumers, we've gone from checking the BBB to now just seeing what others say on Google. Truthfully, we are pretty limited on ways to check on the viability of a business. Let me ask you this — if you were a bank and you wanted to help homeowners finance a roof, you'd want to make sure you got paid back, right? So you'd definitely want to make sure you only loan money to people whose roofing companies are solid and do good work — otherwise, you risk people not wanting to pay. Make sense?"

Source: Day 1 Retail Training, page 40

4. Vetted by 3rd Party Finance

Control Point Finance Companies Did The Vetting We Can't

"Well, another feather in our cap is we are also one of just a few companies licensed by the State of Florida to offer direct-to-consumer financing. In fact, some of these finance companies that have agreed to put their name next to ours took over three months to approve us. They have looked at our finances, our install record, our record with the State, and so on. The good news is they did your homework for you in vetting us out. Even if you didn't finance, you would agree that doing business with a company that can pass that kind of rigorous background check is better than a chuck in a truck, right? When protecting everything you own and everyone you love, is the cheapest the most important thing — or is getting the best value for your money important?"

Source: Day 1 Retail Training, page 41 (timing updated to current: 3+ months)
