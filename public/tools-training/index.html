Tool 1 of 3 · CRM

JobNimbus

The source of truth for every homeowner and every job. For most of the inspection flow, JN is hands-off — but when you convert a retail-cert customer to an appointment, this is where you do it.

When do you actually need to be in JobNimbus?
  • Free Roof Inspection flow: You don't need to go to JN. Everything is done automatically — the signed agreement, the inspection result (Damage / Retail / No Damage), the photos, the certificate PDF — all flow into JN by themselves.
  • Converting a retail-cert customer to an appointment: This is when you go into JN. See the walkthrough below.

How JobNimbus is organized (so the next part makes sense)

Everything in JobNimbus flows through three layers:

1. Contact = the homeowner
A person. Has a name, address, phone, email. Everything starts here.
2. Job = the roof on that homeowner's house
We create a job for that contact. EVERYTHING from this point forward is done through that job — photos, documents, status changes, appointments. If you're working a homeowner, you should be inside their job.
3. Task = an appointment, follow-up, or to-do inside the job
An appointment is just a task inside the job. So is a callback, a measurement order, or a "drop off keys" reminder. All of it lives on the job.

The one workflow you need: Setting an appointment with a retail-cert customer

You went to a retail-cert customer (someone whose roof came back as Retail from the free inspection). They want a real quote. You set an appointment for tomorrow at 2 PM. Here's the JN side:

  1. Open JobNimbus.
  2. In the search bar, type the address. The homeowner will populate.
  3. Click on them.
  4. Make sure you are in the JOB (not the contact). The job is the second level — if you're on the contact page, click into the job underneath.
  5. Click Task.
  6. Set the appointment — tomorrow, 2 PM. Add a title (e.g. "Retail quote sit") and save.
Why search by address (not name)? Addresses are unique; names aren't. Two Smiths on the same street will trip you up. Address gets you to the right homeowner in one search every time.
⚠ Common pitfalls
  • Working on the Contact instead of the Job. If you set a task on the Contact, it doesn't tie to the work — make sure the URL or page header says you're inside the JOB.
  • Creating a new job when the homeowner already has one. The Free Roof Inspection flow already created the job. Always search by address first — never start from "+ New."
  • Touching JN during the inspection flow. You don't need to. Signing, result, photos, cert — all automatic. Doing it manually creates duplicates that the office has to clean up.

Getting set up

  1. IT provisions your @shingleusa.com email + JN login. Check your work email for the invite.
  2. Install the JN mobile app — iOS or Android. Sign in.
  3. Run through the appointment walkthrough above with Neal on a real (or test) retail-cert customer.

Other things JobNimbus does (for reference)

You'll touch most of these later, not on Day 1. They're here so you know what JN's full surface looks like.

Lead tracking + Sales boards
Unified board view of every lead and job in the pipeline. Managers use this heavily; reps less often.
Photos & annotations
Capture and annotate (draw circles, arrows). For Free Roof Inspections, photos auto-upload from the inspector app — you don't add them manually.
Engage texting
Text the homeowner directly from JN. Conversation history saves to the contact.
Job share
Send the homeowner a live link to see their job status (permits pulled, install date, photos).
Notes + @mentions
Drop notes on a job, @mention office staff or crew to loop them in.
Integrated measurements (EagleView, HOVER, Roofr)
Order a measurement directly from the JN job. The report comes back attached automatically.
Proposals with digital signatures
Send a proposal by email or sign in-app on the iPad. Mostly for once you're past the appointment stage.
Insights reporting
Performance analytics + leaderboards. Mostly a manager view.
Tool 2 of 3 · Where to knock

RepCard

Your map of where to knock. The office uploads the lead data; you open the app, filter it down to what you want to work, and update the status as you go.

What you need to know about RepCard at U.S. Shingle
  • We upload the data. You don't capture leads with NFC cards or run follow-up drips — the homeowners on your map are pre-loaded by the office. Your job is just to work them.
  • Two things you actually need to learn: (1) how to filter the map to today's leads, and (2) what the status pins mean + how to update one when you knock.

Workflow 1: Filter the map to today's leads

When you open RepCard you land on a map of your territory. Orange house pins are individual homeowners. Numbered circles are clusters — zoom in to see the individual pins. Working ALL pins is overwhelming; filter first.

  1. Open RepCard. You're on the map.
  2. Tap the Filter button on the right-side toolbar (the blue funnel icon).
  3. Set your filters:
    • Date — Today, This Week, This Month, etc. Stick to fresh leads.
    • Status — tap "Select Status" → pick the pin color you want to see (most common: NEED for fresh leads who haven't been knocked yet).
  4. Tap Apply. The map redraws with only matching pins.
  5. Now you know exactly where to go today. Pick a pin, drive to it, knock.

Workflow 2: The status pins — what they mean

Every house on the map has a colored pin showing its current status. Here's the full list of statuses you'll see (taken straight from the Select Status screen). Categories are Lead (not closed yet — most of what you work) and Customer (signed).

🔵 +   NEW
New lead. Never touched. Lead
🟧 🏠   NEED
Need — they need a roof. This is the fresh "go knock these" status. Most of your day works NEED pins. Lead
⚪ 🚪   NH
Not Home. You knocked, nobody answered. Lead
⚫ 👎   NI
Not Interested. Homeowner declined. Lead
🔵 ←   GB
Go Back. Worth a return visit — busy at the door, asked you to come back, etc. Lead
🟢 📅   APPT
Appointment booked. The sit is on the calendar. Lead
🔴 ⭐   IQ
Instant Quote. Homeowner pulled an instant quote (likely via Roofr's Instant Estimator). Lead
🟧 🎁   TILE
Barrel Tile — house has tile roof (different product, different conversation). Lead
🟢 $   SIGN
Signed. Customer signed an agreement. Customer
🔵 🐛   BUY
Buyers. Lead

Workflow 3: Update a pin after you knock

The data is only useful if it's current. After every door, update the pin so the map reflects reality.

  1. Tap the house pin on the map. A drawer slides up showing the homeowner's name + address + your distance to them.
  2. Make sure you're on the Lead Status tab (the default — you'll only use Customer Status after a signing).
  3. Tap the status circle that matches what just happened:
    • They opened, want to talk → APPT or NEED+book it in JN
    • Nobody answered → NH
    • "Not today" → NI or GB
  4. Add notes if relevant (dog in the yard, prefers Saturday, etc.).
  5. Done. The pin color changes on the map for everyone.
⚠ Common pitfalls
  • Knocking without filtering first. The map shows hundreds of pins — you'll wander randomly without a plan. Always filter to NEED + Today/Week before you leave the truck.
  • Not updating the pin after a door. If you don't change NEED → NH after a no-answer, you'll re-knock it tomorrow. The whole team will. Update before you walk to the next house.
  • Mixing up Lead Status and Customer Status tabs. 99% of the time you're on Lead Status. Customer Status is only for signed-and-converted homeowners.
  • Working stale pins. If a NEED pin was uploaded 6 months ago and never touched, the homeowner has likely moved on. Filter by date (This Week, This Month) to focus on recent uploads.

Getting set up

  1. Manager sends you a RepCard invite + adds you to the team account.
  2. Install the RepCard mobile app. Sign in.
  3. Verify you can see your assigned territory on the map (pins should be visible).
  4. Practice the Filter → NEED → Today flow with Neal before your first day in the field.
Tool 3 of 3 · Measurements + proposals

Roofr

Order an accurate roof measurement in 2 hours for $13. Build a professional proposal in minutes. Close on the spot.

What it is

An aerial roof measurement service + proposal builder + invoicing tool, designed for roofing reps. You stop guessing squares from the ground and the homeowner sees a polished proposal that's tied to real measurements.

Daily-use features

Measurement Reports
"$13 USD to get a report delivered within 2 hours" — includes roof area, facets, pitch, eaves, valleys, hips, and an auto-generated materials list. Order from the address; the report is back before your in-home sit.
Instant Estimator
"Instant roof estimates based on custom pricing that homeowners can access via ads, websites, and door knockers." Plug your pricing in once, and the homeowner can get a ballpark from a link before you even sit them.
Proposals
"Professional, fully customizable proposals that empower you to win jobs faster." Templated but personal — your logo, your pricing, the homeowner's photos, your warranty terms. Build in minutes, present, sign on the spot.
Customer-facing visuals
Aerial roof imagery built into the estimate. The homeowner SEES the roof while you walk through the proposal — much more convincing than abstract square-footage talk.
Payments & Invoicing
"Create, send, and track digital invoices with automated reminders." Credit card + ACH processing built in. Reduces 30-day "where's my check?" follow-up.
Roofr CRM (Kanban board)
"All-in-one management with customizable job board stages." Pipeline view filtered by rep, value, date. Use this OR JobNimbus — we use JobNimbus, but Roofr's CRM exists if you ever need a second view.
Supplier integrations
ABC Supply, CertainTeed, and others. Material lists from your measurement can be priced directly against supplier rates.
U.S. Shingle workflow notes — we use Roofr primarily for the measurement report (the $13 / 2-hour report) and the proposal builder. Pricing is preloaded by the office; do not change it without Neal's approval. Proposals go through Neal for review before they hit a homeowner on the first few sits.
⚠ Common pitfalls
  • Ordering the measurement only AFTER the homeowner asks for the quote — order it BEFORE the sit so you walk in with it ready
  • Submitting a wrong address for measurement — the report comes back for someone else's roof and you wasted $13. Verify by satellite first
  • Editing the pricing on the fly to "win" the deal. Talk to Neal first — we have a low-bid match guarantee, you don't have to discount
  • Forgetting to save the proposal PDF back to JN's Documents tab — keep everything for that homeowner in one place

Getting set up

  1. Manager sends you a Roofr invite + adds you to the team account.
  2. Install the Roofr mobile app (or use the web app — both work).
  3. Sign in. Verify you can see the team's pricing templates.
  4. Order a practice measurement on your own address — see the workflow end-to-end before you do it for a real homeowner.

How they work together

A normal day in the field touches all three tools. Here's the typical chain:

RepCard

1. Knock the door

Hand over (or tap) your RepCard. Homeowner's info captured automatically; they get your card on their phone.

RepCard → JN

2. Book the sit

Disposition the lead in RepCard. Sit booked → syncs to JobNimbus as a new contact + scheduled appointment.

Roofr

3. Pre-sit measurement

Before the in-home sit, order a Roofr measurement on the address. Report back in 2 hours.

Roofr

4. Build the proposal

Use the measurement + team pricing template to build a proposal. Print or load it on the iPad for the sit.

JobNimbus

5. Run the in-home

Take before-photos with JN's camera. Present the proposal. If signed, JN marks the job sold.

JobNimbus

6. Handoff to install

Job sits in JN under the right status. Office schedules the install. Photos + signed docs + measurement all live on the job for the crew.
