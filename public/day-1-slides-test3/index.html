Why a small yes works · the 4 drivers

Self-Perception (Bem) — "I'm someone who takes action on my home."
Cognitive Dissonance (Festinger) — saying no now makes step one feel meaningless.
Commitment & Consistency (Cialdini) — stickiest when public, voluntary & written.
Sunk Cost — once they've invested time/emotion, walking away feels like a loss.

Florida · the 15-year letter

"CHANGE YOUR ROOF —
OR WE'RE DROPPING YOU."

If the insurer drops them, the mortgage company forces its own policy on the home — their terms, their price.
The bill: ~$2,200/yr → ~$8,000/yr. 3–4× overnight.
Worst part: forced-place covers the structure only. Not the furniture, electronics, or family photos.

A small yes must do 3 things

1. Cost them nothing — no money, no commitment, no risk.
2. Solve THEIR problem — not pitch ours.
3. Be an added option — never take away what they already have.
"Would you rather have just the option of paying out of pocket — or the added option of a report that makes them leave you alone?"
"But you are free to accept or refuse." (Guéguen) — roughly doubles compliance.

Wear & tear · 32% · the Go-Back

YES 4 TIMES BEFORE YOU ASK

Yes #1 — anchor: "No plans of moving in the next year or two — right?" Now the roof is their problem.
Yes #2 — ethics: "A company's ethics & standing matter to you — right?" Nobody says no. That's the trap.
Yes #3 — trap springs: "I'd love to say it has 5 years left — but that'd be unethical, and we don't operate that way. You understand, right?"
Walk the report: "Not a scare tactic… although the leak you don't see is the worst kind."
Yes #4 — the pivot: "Since you're not moving, you'll have to address this soon — right?" Only question left is when.

Why now, not later

Cheapest today. Storm season adds cost; two supplier price hikes since January. Waiting could cost thousands more.
A new roof drops insurance — shop it after and save up to 30%+. Thousands back.
"You're free to accept or refuse — replace now, save a lot; replace later, lose a lot. Kind of that simple."

Why people buy · the 4 impulse factors

F.I.G.S.

F — Fear of Loss: here today, gone tomorrow; it won't always be available.
I — Indifference: so confident in it you're willing to walk away.
G — Greed: people want what others have. Keeping up with the Joneses.
S — Sense of Urgency: "I'm only in your area for a short while."
