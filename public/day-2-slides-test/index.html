Presentation · The warm-up

THE WARM-UP

  • 80% of the roofing sale is won or lost in the warm-up. Get it right and the close takes care of itself; rush it and nothing else you say matters.
  • The warm-up is Slides 1–5, and they all do one job: establish and reaffirm our Experience and Credibility.
  • Why those two above everything else? Because experience + credibility are what give the homeowner peace of mind — and peace of mind is what they're really buying. Price always comes second to "can I trust these people with the thing protecting everyone I love?"
  • And you build all of it the same way — through control points.

Presentation · Control points

WHAT ARE CONTROL POINTS

  • A control point is a small, deliberate question that earns a quick "yes" and hands the lead of the conversation back to you.
  • They're the "right?" · "wouldn't you agree?" · "fair enough?" · "make sense?" · "you understand that, right?" built into every slide on purpose.
  • Each "yes" is a micro-commitment. Stack enough of them and the final close is just one more yes at the end of a long line of them.
  • They also keep you leading — you're the one asking and steering, instead of standing there being interrogated about price.
  • Hear them in Slides 1–5: "…the likelihood of us being around is extremely high, wouldn't you agree?" · "You want a fully licensed contractor, right?" · "You aren't opposed to having more coverage, are you?" · "Experience matters, doesn't it?"

ENERGY SAVER PACKAGE

Speaking of radiant heat, what would you say your average electric bill is in the summer? Now how about in the winter? Ok so then overall you'd say your average bill is about ________?

In the summer attic temperatures can reach 150 degrees or higher. Ever been up there in the summer? You have like 12 seconds and then you can't breathe. Well, what that means is that you are PAYING, with your hard-earned money, to cool the air to 50 degrees because you're about to pipe it up into the furnace so that when it finally comes out over here it's 74 degrees. The solution is 3 parts. First is to add more insulation as most homes don't have anywhere near enough for our Florida heat. We install R-38 or about this much (use your hands to show about 15") compared to what most people have which is this much (use your hands to show about 4"). The second is to seal the ducts you have in the attic. Neither the builder nor the guy who installed your AC were concerned about your electric bill — they were more interested in doing the job quickly to get paid and move on. That means your ducts were never sealed properly, so the air you are paying to cool isn't making it into the house but instead getting lost up there. So we will seal and reseal them using sealing mastic. The other issue with improperly sealed ducts is air quality — the return ducts are sucking in dust, pollen, mold and anything else floating around up there.

The third part, which is the most CRITICAL, is something called Radiant Barrier. It's high-tech stuff that literally reflects 97% of the sun's radiating heat from ever entering your attic. It was invented by NASA for the space shuttle. So now instead of having to pay to cool your air to 50 degrees (scroll back) you only need to cool it to about 70 degrees instead (scroll forward), because we will have dramatically reduced the temperature in your attic.

This will not only save you up to 40% or more off your utility bill but it will also extend the life of your AC system. If the AC is running half as much it's going to last longer, isn't it?

Obviously if you have it already, you're good to go, but I've only seen one home so far that had a radiant barrier installed. Let me just check first and then I can give you the breakdown and the investment for just the roof and then what it looks like for the whole package.
