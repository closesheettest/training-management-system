Here in Florida

THE 15-YEAR LETTER

When a roof hits 15 years old or older, the homeowner can get a letter from their insurance company that says —
"Change your roof — or we're dropping you."

Why that matters

WHY IS THAT SIGNIFICANT?

If the insurance company drops them — their mortgage company steps in.
They put forced-place insurance on the home.
Mortgage company's policy. Their terms. Their price.

What that does to their bill

THE COST SHOCK

Not their family photos. Not anything inside the four walls.

The flow

FROM FIRST INTERACTION TO INSPECTION

1 —Your first interaction is with a pre-qualified homeowner: 15+ year roof, owner lives there.
2 —Get the small yes — a free roof inspection.
3 —The company inspects the roof. Your job ends at the inspection.

Every inspection lands one of three ways

THREE OUTCOMES

Storm damage — a Public Adjuster files the claim and fights the carrier. New roof at no out-of-pocket cost.
No damage — they get a certified inspection report: ammunition against the insurance letter.
Retail — we show them the wear & tear and offer a paid or financed replacement.

Storm damage path

ENTER THE PUBLIC ADJUSTER.

A state-licensed pro who represents the homeowner — not the insurance company.
The insurance company has their adjuster. The homeowner gets ours.
They file the claim, supply the documentation, handle the carrier — and push until it lands.

How you get paid

APPROVED AND INSTALLED.

Not when you got the inspection. Not when the claim was filed.
You earn when the insurance approves the claim and we install the roof.
It's a chain — set the expectation now: you get paid when the chain finishes.
