Day 3 — the shift

Verbatim is OUT.

Your voice is IN.

Day 1-2 was about getting the words in your head. Today you start making it sound like you — your phrasing, your rhythm, your personality.

But the information stays in and the structure stays in. If you skip the importance question, the "why?", the control point, or the summary-back — the pitch doesn't work.

The drill

Pairs · 5 min per round · switch

  • One of you is the rep. One is the homeowner.
  • Rep delivers the pitch in your own words — your phrasing, your delivery.
  • Homeowner: don't be easy. Push back. Ask why. Be skeptical.
  • End of 5 min — swap roles and run it again.
  • Between rounds: look at the slide. Did you cover every piece? Did you hit the control point? Did you ask the "why?"

The script on screen is a checklist, not a teleprompter. If you covered every beat in your own words, you nailed it.

Drill 1 · in your voice

Free Roof Inspection — beats to hit

The door pitch. Cover every beat below — phrasing is yours.

"Hi, my name is __________. I'm stopping by because we sent you something in the mail in regards to your roof.

We sent this to you because according to county records you are at high risk of the insurance company dropping you JUST BECAUSE OF THE AGE OF YOUR ROOF!

Let me ask you one question — if you were to get the letter tomorrow from the insurance, would you rather have just the option of coming out of pocket to replace your roof, or would you like the added option of showing them an inspection report where they would leave you alone?

Great — that's exactly why I'm here. We're here to get you scheduled for a no-cost roof inspection. If there's no damage, we'll provide you an official inspection certificate that says you have 5+ years of life left in your roof. This will make them leave you alone."

Drill 1 · bridge

3 things that can happen

  1. No damage — you're protected against the letter (we issue the certificate).
  2. Wear & tear — no cert, but we have many options for you (this is where you flip to the Go-Back script).
  3. Storm damage — a public adjuster fights to get insurance to pay, no out-of-pocket cost.

Drill 2 · Setup

Retail Go-Back — Setup beats

When wear & tear is found. Hit both "right?" questions and the ethics framing in your own words.

"Hi __________, I have your report here for you. Before we go over it — a couple quick questions.

You don't have any plans of moving in the next year or two, right?

And when you do business with anyone, the company's ethics and standing is important to you, right?

Great — I was hoping you'd answer that way. I'd love to be able to give you a report that says your roof has 5 more years of life left in it. But if I did, that would be unethical — and we just don't operate that way. You understand, right?

Here, let me show you what you have going on up there…"

Drill 2 · Walk + pivot

Go-Back — Walk the report

(Go over the report.)

"As poor a shape as your roof is, this isn't a scare tactic. I'm not saying you absolutely must replace your roof right now, or terrible things are going to happen. Although — the leak you don't see is actually the worst kind.

Since we know you're not moving anytime soon, you'd agree at some point soon, this is something you're going to have to address, right?

Here are the two main reasons to consider doing it now —

1. Today is the cheapest you'll ever pay for a new roof. Storm season is coming. Since January, two price increases from our suppliers. Waiting a year could literally cost you thousands more.

2. Insurance. Once you replace your roof, you can shop your insurance and realize savings up to 30% or more."

Drill 2 · Close

Go-Back — Close + Ask

"Of course, you are free to accept or refuse the advice. I just wanted you to know and understand the options and what's in front of you.

Replace it now, save a lot of money. Replace it later, lose a lot of money. Really kind of that simple."

Both decision-makers home → do the pitch. Only one → schedule the second appt.

"Let me set a time to come back tomorrow — I still need to look in the attic. Is the afternoon or evening better, when you'll both be home?"

Drill 3 · in your voice

In-Home Pitch · Slides 1-16

Last night you memorized the words. Now own them.

  • Slides 1-5 — the 4 talking points (15 Years, Veteran, 5-Star, 3rd Party Finance). Cover each one with: importance question → why → summary using your control point. Three beats, your phrasing.
  • Slides 6-16 — the walk through credibility into the product framing. Keep the order, find your rhythm.

Checklist between rounds: open /day-2-homework/. Did you cover every talking point? Did you hit the "why?" on each one?

Control points · don't skip this

The "why?" question is non-negotiable.

  • Ask the importance question.
  • They short-answer: "it's important." (Normal.)
  • You: "Why is that important to you?"
  • They give you the real reasoning.
  • You summarize back using your Control Point as the words you put in their mouth.

Now they said it.

Every deal. Always in order. Section 1 to 5 is the muscle memory.

Section 1

Create the Contact

  • Open JN → Contacts tab → + New Contact
  • Name, address, phone, email — same info you collected at the door
  • Location field — set to the homeowner's city, OR "test" if it's a practice deal
  • Save → you land on the Contact's detail page (this is where Section 2 starts)

The Contact is the person. The Job (next) is the roof on that house.

Section 2

Create the Job

  • From the Contact's page, scroll to JOBS → tap + Create New
  • Job is auto-linked to the Contact
  • Fill in Job Name (we'll use the address) → Save
  • You'll come back to this Job after the sale to fill in Edit Job fields (Section 5)

One Contact can have multiple Jobs if the homeowner owns multiple properties.

Section 3

Create the Appointment

  • From the Job page → Appointments section → + Create
  • Pick the date + time you booked at the door
  • Type: Inspection (first appt) or Estimate Presentation (go-back)
  • This auto-syncs to the office's calendar — they know you're going

No appointment in JN means the office doesn't know it exists. Always log it.

Section 4

Build the Estimate

  • From the Job page → Estimates → + Create Estimate
  • Layout — pick the U.S. Shingle template
  • Price Details → open the product card (e.g. "Exposed Fastener · 11 items") → set Quantity and Price for each line
  • Add roof, RB, insulation as separate line items (we'll do this together in the practice deal tonight)
  • Sign Now → the homeowner signs on your phone → ✓ checkmark saves it

Roofing = squares. Insulation + Radiant Barrier = square feet. Don't mix them up.

Section 5

Fill out the Edit Job fields

  • After the estimate is signed → back to the Job → tap Edit
  • Fill every field in order: Sold Date, Payment Type (Upgrade), Roof Price ONLY, # of Squares, # of Stories, Color, Product toggles, RB sqft + cost, Insulation sqft + cost
  • Skip everything past the product block — permits, NOC, Hannah, "DO NOT TOUCH" are all office territory
  • Tap ✓ in top right to save

🚨 NEVER set the Start Date or End Date when editing the job — those are the office's to set. Leave them blank.

The office reads these fields to order material, schedule the crew, and bill correctly. Don't skip any of the ones above.

JN gotchas to remember

  • Roof Price ONLY = the roof line only. Don't include RB or insulation in that field — they have their own fields below.
  • RB / Insulation costs = TOTAL dollars, not per-sqft. If RB is 3,000 sqft × $2.75, enter $8,250, not 2.75.
  • Color, # of Stories = if you miss these, the office can't order material.
  • Location = "test" for practice deals. Tonight's homework uses this.
  • 🚨 Start Date / End Date = NEVER set these when editing the job. They're the office's. Leave them blank.

Tool 2

RepCard

  • Install → sign in with your @shingleusa.com email
  • Map view shows your zone + the houses you've tapped
  • Tap a house → log the result: Not Home, Pitched, Inspection Booked, Sold
  • Your zone manager sees this in real time

Doors-knocked + bookings auto-flow to your weekly report.

Tool 3

Roofr

  • Order a measurement report on any address — comes back within ~30 minutes
  • Report shows the roof from every angle + the waste % table
  • The only number you need: the recommended waste % (usually 7%)
  • Use that to size the deal in your estimate (Section 4)

Saves you climbing the roof to measure. Office pays for the report; you just order it.
