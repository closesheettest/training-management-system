Morning · Roleplay

Why we do this.

The script doesn't live on a piece of paper — it lives in your mouth. The first time you say it out loud it sounds robotic. The fifth time it sounds like you. The fiftieth time it sounds like you've always said it that way.

Two hours from now you'll have said each of these scripts to another human at least six times. That's how this starts becoming muscle memory.

How to roleplay well

1 · Be the homeowner
React the way a real homeowner would. Bored, busy, suspicious, curious — pick a flavor and commit.
2 · Don't read
Look at the script once. Set it down. Try the lines from memory. Re-check only when you get stuck.
3 · Give real feedback
"That was great" doesn't help. "Slow down on the address-it line — you blew past the yes" does.

Rotate partners every round. Different reactions stress-test different parts of the script.

Free Roof Inspection Script

What we're drilling: the open, the high-risk-of-being-dropped line, the "just the option of coming out of pocket / added option of an inspection report" ask, and the close — verbatim. By the end of the round, your partner should be able to repeat the ask back to you, word for word.

Reference: Day 1 slide 30.

Free Roof Inspection Script · verbatim

"Hi, my name is __________. I'm stopping by because we sent you something in the mail in regards to your roof.

We sent this to you because according to county records you are at high risk of the insurance company dropping you JUST BECAUSE OF THE AGE OF YOUR ROOF!

Let me ask you one question — if you were to get the letter tomorrow from the insurance, would you rather have just the option of coming out of pocket to replace your roof, or would you like the added option of showing them an inspection report where they would leave you alone?

Great — that's exactly why I'm here. We're here to get you scheduled for a no-cost roof inspection. If there's no damage, we'll provide you an official inspection certificate that says you have 5+ years of life left in your roof. This will make them leave you alone."

Roleplay 2 of 3

Retail Roof Go-Back Script

What we're drilling: the four-yes chain. Moving question → ethics question → ethics-trap reveal → "you'll have to address it" pivot. By the time you ask for the appointment, the homeowner should have said yes four times without realizing it.

Reference: Day 1 slides 31c – 31e.

Retail Go-Back · open + ethics trap

"Hi __________, I have your report here for you. Before we go over it — a couple quick questions.

You don't have any plans of moving in the next year or two, right?

And when you do business with anyone, the company's ethics and standing is important to you, right?

Great — I was hoping you'd answer that way. I'd love to be able to give you a report that says your roof has 5 more years of life left in it. But if I did, that would be unethical — and we just don't operate that way. You understand, right?

Here, let me show you what you have going on up there…"

Retail Go-Back · pivot + close

(Go over the report.)

"As poor a shape as your roof is, this isn't a scare tactic. Although — the leak you don't see is actually the worst kind.

Since we know you're not moving, you'd agree at some point soon, this is something you're going to have to address, right?

Two main reasons to consider doing it now: 1. Today is the cheapest you'll ever pay. Storm season is coming, two price increases since January. 2. Insurance — a new roof can drop your premium 30%+.

Of course, you are free to accept or refuse the advice. Replace it now, save a lot of money. Replace it later, lose a lot of money. Really kind of that simple.

Okay — let me set a time to come back tomorrow, I still need to look in the attic as well. Is the afternoon or evening better, when you'll both be home?"

"What we are going to do tonight is tell you a little bit about who we are as a company, take photos of everything, and show you all the options and types of materials that are available to you — while guaranteeing a 30-day price.

Afternoon · Products

Now — what we actually sell.

Up to now, your job has been to get the appointment. Once you're at the kitchen table, you need to know your products cold — what they cost, what they last, what they're rated for, and which ones get the insurance company to leave the homeowner alone.

The framework

Every product gets pitched both ways.

Without Energy Package
The product
Just the roof. Wind rating, warranty, and base benefits.
With Energy Package
The roof + savings
Radiant barrier, R-38, duct sealing — the whole envelope upgrade.

The main focus: both sides should look the same size in your pitch.

Every product carries these — always.

  • High wind resistance
  • Premium secondary water barrier
  • Veteran-owned company in business over 15 years
  • 10-year workmanship no-leak warranty
  • Transferable when they sell the home

These 5 lines are the floor. If they're not in your pitch, you're under-selling the company before you even get to the material.

The Energy Package

The upgrade — everything from standard, plus:

  • Radiant Barrier 97% — bounces heat back outside
  • Insulation R-38
  • Duct sealing or re-sealing
  • Lifetime products
  • Extends the life of their HVAC system
  • Improves indoor air quality

This is what flips the conversation from "a roof" to "an envelope upgrade." Pitch it on every product.

When you pitch a product —

  1. Name the product
  2. The specs (wind, warranty, pitch range)
  3. The insurance angle if it applies (metal = leaves alone, asphalt = can still drop)
  4. The 5 shared benefits
  5. Without Energy Package — what the price gets them
  6. With Energy Package — same product, plus the envelope upgrade

Both energy options should look the same size in your pitch. Equal weight.
