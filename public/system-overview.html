1. What the system does

The Training Management System replaces a manual chain of texts, spreadsheets, emails, and follow-ups with one place that runs an entire training week on autopilot. From the moment a new hire is enrolled by HR, the system handles:

One-off "attendance-only" classes. Not every class on the schedule is a training week. Sometimes it's a one-day company meeting where Neal just needs headcount tracking. When the "📋 One-off meeting — attendance only" toggle is checked at create time, the class skips every automation: no registration texts, no provisioning, no itinerary email, no hotels, no final test, no graduation report, no welcome drip, no social posts. The Class detail page hides every workflow card except the attendee roster + kiosk link, and the Schedule page tags the class with an amber "📋 Attendance only" pill so it's clearly different from real training weeks. For meetings with a lot of people (e.g. a 70-person company meeting), the Class detail page exposes a "📋 Bulk import (CSV)" button — upload a CSV file or paste a list of attendees and every one gets created at once.
Role-based views. When someone opens the app, a "Who are you?" splash asks them to pick their name (from the Notifications list). The nav then shows only the pages that role uses — IT sees Provisioning, HR sees Hotels + Schedule, the Hiring Manager sees their enrollment + hotel pages, and so on. Admins see everything. Pages outside a role's view are also blocked at the URL level — typing the URL directly shows a "Not in your view" screen with a Switch button. Configurable on the{' '} Personas page (Settings ▾). This is role-based gating, not authentication — there's no password and anyone can switch personas to gain different access.
  • Trainee on-boarding — the trainee gets a registration text with their personal link; if they don't respond in 24 hours the system auto-sends a friendly follow-up, and a final reminder 48 hours after that. They can also tap a "Can't make it" link on the registration page to cleanly opt out, which alerts HR and stops further texts. All three registration texts are editable from the Message Templates page.
  • Daily kiosk check-in — a tablet at the training site captures attendance for every day of the class.
  • Company-email provisioning — on day 2, IT gets a text telling them to create the Google Workspace accounts. They download a pre-filled CSV with one click and bulk-upload it.
  • Platform setup — the moment IT marks provisioning complete, HR and a Virtual Assistant (VA) are texted/emailed with a checklist for setting up each trainee in RepCard, JobNimbus, and Sales Academy.
  • Credentials delivery — once setup is done, the corporate trainer sends each trainee a text with their email, password, and step-by-step iPhone/Android setup instructions.
  • Day-of safety nets — hotel no-show alerts at 10:30 AM so unused rooms can be cancelled; dropout detection that pings IT and HR to clean up accounts.
  • End-of-week reporting — a PDF graduating class report is generated and emailed automatically once every trainee has submitted their final test.
  • Auto-marketing & handoff — every trainee gets a post-test review-request email asking for 4 reviews (Google + Yelp for U.S. Shingle, Google + Yelp for Neal Scoppettuolo — Corporate Trainer), each pre-filled with one of their own essay answers. They also get a text with a vCard link that saves the team's handoff contacts (Sales Manager, Helpline, etc.) to their phone in one tap. A class graduation announcement fires immediately to Facebook + LinkedIn, and a paced testimonial queue posts one trainee quote per platform per day to keep social alive between classes.

The dollar value is in the time saved per class week — what used to be an inbox-driven, "who-texted-who" workflow is now visible, dedup'd, and self-driving.

2. The people involved

Each role has clearly defined responsibilities, and each gets pinged only for the things they own. People are added once on the Notifications page; from there they can be checked into specific events.

Admin / Operations
Adds locations, schedules class weeks, enrolls trainees, manages the whole system. Sees everything.
Hiring Manager
Enrolls new trainees into upcoming class weeks. Adds candidate names, phone numbers, and answers a forced Yes/No on "Needs hotel" so HR knows up front who needs a room.
IT
Creates company emails in Google Workspace from a CSV. Gets pinged on day 2.
HR
Notified when emails are ready; oversees that the VA gets the setup list. Also handles hotel no-show alerts.
Virtual Assistant (VA)
Sets up each trainee in RepCard, JobNimbus, and Sales Academy. Marks each platform done per trainee.
Corporate Trainer
Runs the class. Sends final test, sends graduating reps their email credentials when everything is ready.

3. A training week, day by day

Before the class — enrollment

Hiring Manager adds new candidates to an upcoming class week. The system automatically texts each one a registration link. The trainee opens it, confirms name, fills in home address + sales experience.

Day 1 — orientation

Trainees arrive at the training location. They sign in at the kiosk on the front-desk tablet. Day 1 is intro / orientation — no system actions fire yet.

Day 2 — provisioning

When the first trainee signs in at the kiosk on day 2 (or at 11 AM as a fallback), IT gets a text and email: "Day 2 trainees have started checking in. Time to create company emails for [region]. Open the Provision page..." IT opens the link, reviews 12 pre-filled rows (one per checked-in trainee, auto-named firstname.lastname@shingleusa.com), clicks Download CSV, uploads it to Google Workspace, then clicks ✅ Mark provisioning complete. That triggers two more notifications, instantly.

Day 2, immediately after IT — HR & VA

HR is texted/emailed that the email list is ready, with a link to the Setup page. The VA is also pinged: "12 new trainees need to be set up in RepCard, JobNimbus, and Sales Academy." The VA opens the link, sees the email list and a checklist for each trainee. They check off each platform per trainee as they go.

Day 2–5 — training, kiosk check-ins, safety nets

Trainees attend each day, signing in at the kiosk each morning. Daily at 10:30 AM, a cron checks for hotel-needing trainees who haven't shown — HR is alerted so they can cancel unused rooms. Daily at 1 PM, the system checks for trainees who were provisioned but didn't show that day; if any look like dropouts, IT is asked to delete their Google Workspace account and HR is asked to remove their app access.

Last day — final test

Trainees take a final assessment from their phone. Multiple-choice + a few essay/testimonial questions. Scores are saved automatically. The moment a trainee taps Submit, three things happen in parallel: (1) a 4-review email lands in their inbox (Google + Yelp for U.S. Shingle, Google + Yelp for Neal Scoppettuolo — Corporate Trainer), each pre-filled with one of their own essay answers so they only click and paste; (2) a text fires with a vCard link that saves their team's handoff contacts (Sales Manager + Helpline) to their phone in one tap; (3) their testimonial-eligible essays are added to the paced social-post queue for the daily 9 AM Eastern flush.

Once everyone has submitted the final test — graduation

Within 15 minutes of the last trainee submitting, the system emails the subscribed leadership a PDF graduating-class report. Subject: "Graduating training week of May 11 – 15, 2026". Attachment: a one-page PDF listing every graduate with their company email and days attended, plus week totals.

Trainer's call — send credentials to reps

Once setup is complete and the trainer is confident the trainees are ready, they trigger the final step: each trainee receives a personal text with their company email, password, and a link explaining how to add the account to their iPhone or Android, plus install instructions for RepCard and JobNimbus.

6. When someone drops out mid-week

If a trainee has been set up with a company email and apps, then doesn't show up to class for the day, the system handles cleanup automatically:

  1. Daily cron at ~1 PM Eastern scans every active class.
  2. For every enrolled trainee who has a company email but no attendance today, the system flags them as a likely dropout.
  3. One summary text + email goes to IT listing the trainee names and their company emails — so IT can delete their Google Workspace accounts.
  4. A parallel text + email goes to HR with the same list — they remove the trainee from RepCard, JobNimbus, and Sales Academy.
  5. The trainee is marked notified so they aren't reported again on subsequent days.

7. End-of-week graduation report

Once every enrolled trainee has submitted their final test, the system generates a PDF report and emails it to whoever is subscribed.

  • Trigger: last test submission detected by a cron that polls every ~15 minutes.
  • Subject: "Graduating training week of May 11 – 15, 2026"
  • Attachment: a one-page Letter PDF with just the graduate count and a numbered roster of name / phone / home address — nothing else (no test scores, no email, no days attended). Leadership uses this to know who graduated and how to reach them.
  • Manual re-send: the Class detail page has a "🎓 Graduation report email" card with a Send / Re-send button. Use it if the auto-cron run errored (Resend outage, sender not yet verified, no subscribers) — the button surfaces the actual delivery result so you can fix root causes.
  • Sent once. Each class fires exactly one graduation report; subsequent cron runs see the timestamp and skip.
  • Graduation social post fires at the same time — a generic "🎓 Just wrapped another training week …" post goes out immediately to the Facebook Page and LinkedIn personal profile (with a random venue photo attached when available). This is separate from the paced testimonial queue described below.

Paced testimonial queue (after graduation)

As each trainee submits their final test, the system also pulls every essay they marked as a testimonial and adds them to a queue instead of posting immediately:

  • The trainee's longest essay goes to the Facebook side of the queue; the second-longest goes to the LinkedIn side. So the same trainee shows up on both networks but with different quotes.
  • If a trainee only has one testimonial-eligible essay, Facebook gets it and LinkedIn is skipped (to avoid posting the same quote twice).
  • A daily 9 AM Eastern cron picks the oldest pending item per platform and publishes it — one Facebook post + one LinkedIn post per day.
  • A 12-trainee class with 2 testimonials each turns into roughly 12 days of paced content on each network instead of 24 simultaneous posts.
  • Posts pair the question (which carries the SEO-friendly keywords like "sales training") with the trainee's verbatim answer. We never reword what the trainee wrote — the SEO benefit comes from the question framing, not from massaging their voice. Same rule applies on the testimonials feed embedded at nealscoppettuolo.com and in the post-test Google/Yelp review email.
  • Generic copy: never mentions the client company name. Location phrasing: venue + city for hotels ("at the Hilton in Orlando"), city only for client HQ ("in St Pete").
  • Live status + "Post next now" override available on /messages.

8. Group broadcasts (ad-hoc SMS & email)

Beyond all the workflow-triggered messages above, the system has a manual broadcast tool at /group-messages for the moments admin wants to talk to a group of reps directly — not tied to a specific class event.

  1. Pick recipients: a single class week (e.g. just next week's trainees) or every enrolled rep across the system. The page shows a live count and warns when some recipients are missing a phone or email.
  2. Pick channels: SMS, email, or both. Each channel has its own body editor.
  3. Pick a saved template (optional): pre-fills the body and email subject. Three are seeded — Update info request — SMS, Update info request — email, and Group meeting reminder — SMS. All editable on /message-templates.
  4. Preview: a live preview substitutes {firstName} and a per-recipient {link} (pointing to /update-info/<their token>) so you see exactly what the first recipient will get before clicking Send.
  5. Send: a confirmation modal shows the final count and channels. After sending, the page reports per-channel sent / failed counts and stamps last_group_message_sent_at on each recipient who got at least one successful send.

The most common use right now is the "please update your info" blast. After bulk-importing reps via CSV, most have a company phone but no personal email or home address. The blast points each rep to their own /update-info/<token> page where they fill in those fields and save. The data flows back into the same trainees row that drives everything else (registration emails, hotel info, graduation reports), so it's a one-and-done cleanup.
